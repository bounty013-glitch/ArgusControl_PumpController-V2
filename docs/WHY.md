# Why

## Why the Name Argus?

Argus is named for awareness.
The purpose of the system is not merely to collect data.
Its purpose is to observe, understand, and communicate what is happening within complex equipment and processes.
The name reflects the belief that visibility alone is insufficient.
A system should help operators, technicians, and managers understand the state of their equipment and make informed decisions with confidence.
Argus exists to turn data into awareness.

## Why Argus Edge Exists

Argus Edge was not created as a startup idea.
It was created as a response to real operational problems encountered in the field.
The original goal was simple:
Provide the visibility, understanding, and control that operators and managers needed but did not have.
Over time, it became clear that the problem was larger than telemetry.
The industry had become accustomed to systems that generated data without providing understanding.
Operators were expected to interpret dozens of values, alarms, screens, and indicators while simultaneously managing complex equipment in demanding environments.
Many systems provided information.
Few provided clarity.
Argus Edge exists to close that gap.

## The Core Problem

Industrial systems often answer the question:
"What is the value?"
Argus is designed to answer:
"What is happening?"
And eventually:
"What should I do about it?"
The goal is not simply to collect data.
The goal is to reduce cognitive load and improve decision-making.
Operators should not need to mentally assemble scattered pieces of information into a coherent picture of system state.
The system should help explain itself.

## The Origin of Argus Edge

Argus Edge was born from practical necessity.
Existing solutions were often:
- Expensive
- Locked down
- Difficult to modify
- Dependent on proprietary systems
- Slow to adapt
- Focused on displays rather than understanding
In many cases, critical equipment could not easily provide the information operators actually needed.
The result was a dependence on experience, tribal knowledge, and guesswork.
Argus Edge was built to change that.
May-haves are assumptions wearing a tuxedo.
Argus exists to replace polished guesswork with observable truth.

## What Makes Argus Different

Argus Edge does not create traditional SCADA systems.
SCADA systems typically focus on collecting and displaying information.
Argus Edge focuses on interpreting, organizing, and communicating information in a way that supports human decision-making.
The objective is not to show more data.
The objective is to show the right information at the right time.
The system should communicate intent, state, health, and context.
A good Argus Edge screen should answer questions before the operator asks them.

## Design Philosophy

Argus Edge follows several foundational beliefs:
- Control should survive display failure.
- Safety should survive network failure.
- Systems should fail gracefully.
- Human-readable information is mandatory.
- State is more important than raw values.
- Understanding is more valuable than visibility.
- Simplicity is preferred over complexity.
- Reliability is preferred over novelty.
Every design decision should support these principles.

## Long-Term Vision

Argus Edge is evolving through several stages:
Data System
-> Shows values
Information System
-> Explains what is happening
Decision Support System
-> Recommends actions
Autonomous Assistant
-> Assists operators while keeping humans in control
The goal is not automation for its own sake.
The goal is to create systems that help people make better decisions with less effort and greater confidence.

## The Standard

Every Argus Edge product, firmware project, interface, controller, and service should ultimately support one objective:
Help people understand what is happening so they can confidently decide what to do next.
